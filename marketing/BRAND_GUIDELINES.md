# Vibe Rated - Brand Guidelines

## 1. Introduction

Welcome to the Vibe Rated brand guidelines. This document is the definitive resource for understanding and executing our brand identity. Our brand is more than just a logo; it's a system of voice, visuals, and values that come together to create a unique and memorable experience. Adhering to these guidelines ensures that we present a consistent, professional, and powerful brand to the world.

Our mission is to **make the world frequency-aware**. Every piece of communication, every design element, and every interaction should contribute to this mission.

---

## 2. Logo

The Vibe Rated logo is the primary visual representation of our brand. It is a symbol of frequency, energy, and measurement. It must be used consistently and correctly in all applications.

### Primary Logo

![Primary Logo](/home/ubuntu/vibe-rated-repo/marketing/assets/logos/vibe-rated-logo-primary.png)

*   **Description:** A stylized purple lightning bolt and frequency waveform on a dark circular background.
*   **Usage:** This is our main logo. It should be used in most instances, including the app icon, social media profiles, and website headers.

### Wordmark

![Wordmark](/home/ubuntu/vibe-rated-repo/marketing/assets/logos/vibe-rated-wordmark.png)

*   **Description:** The words "VIBE RATED" in a bold, modern, geometric sans-serif font.
*   **Usage:** Use when the brand name needs to be clearly legible, such as in document headers or on merchandise.

### Logo Usage Rules

*   **Clear Space:** Always maintain a clear space around the logo equal to the height of the lightning bolt. This ensures its visibility and impact.
*   **Minimum Size:** The primary logo should never be smaller than 32x32 pixels. The wordmark should never be smaller than 100 pixels wide.
*   **Don'ts:**
    *   Do not stretch, squash, or distort the logo in any way.
    *   Do not change the colors of the logo.
    *   Do not place the logo on a cluttered or visually competing background.
    *   Do not use any part of the logo as a standalone element, unless it is the approved icon variation.

## 4. Typography

Typography is our brand's voice made visible. Our typographic choices are modern, clean, and easy to read.

### Primary Typeface: **Inter**

*   **Usage:** Used for all headlines, body text, and UI elements.
*   **Weights:**
    *   **Bold:** For headlines.
    *   **SemiBold:** For subheadings.
    *   **Regular:** For body text.

### Secondary Typeface: **Roboto Mono**

*   **Usage:** Used exclusively for numerical data, such as vibe scores, decibel levels, and lux values. This creates a clear distinction between descriptive text and objective data.
*   **Weights:** Regular.

### Typographic Scale

*   **H1 (Page Titles):** Inter Bold, 48px
*   **H2 (Section Titles):** Inter Bold, 36px
*   **H3 (Subheadings):** Inter SemiBold, 24px
*   **Body:** Inter Regular, 16px
*   **Data:** Roboto Mono Regular, 16px

## 5. Brand Voice

Our voice is the **Frequency Alchemist**: confident, curious, and slightly mystical, but always grounded in data.

### Key Characteristics

*   **Confident & Knowing:** We speak with the authority of data.
*   **Curious & Playful:** We invite users on a journey of discovery.
*   **Mystical & Aspirational:** We hint at the deeper, unseen world of energy.
*   **Empowering & Direct:** We give users the tools to control their environment.

### Do's and Don'ts

*   **DO:** Use frequency/energy language (e.g., "tune in," "resonate").
*   **DO:** Mix data with feeling (e.g., "89 vibe score = pure flow state").
*   **DO:** Be playful with science (e.g., "17 seconds to decode the matrix").
*   **DON'T:** Be overly technical or academic.
*   **DON'T:** Use generic, corporate language.
*   **DON'T:** Sound preachy or judgmental.

## 6. Imagery & Visuals

Our visual style is moody, atmospheric, and futuristic. It should feel like a glimpse into a world that is both familiar and slightly magical.

### Photography Style

*   **Lighting:** High contrast, with an emphasis on natural light and shadow.
*   **Subject:** Empty or sparsely populated spaces with a strong sense of atmosphere. POV shots are encouraged.
*   **Color Grading:** Cool tones, with our brand purples and indigos used as accent colors in the lighting.
*   **Authenticity:** Avoid staged, stock-photo-like images. We want real spaces with real vibes.

### Visual Motifs

*   **Waveforms:** The most direct representation of frequency. Use them in animations, backgrounds, and graphic elements.
*   **Concentric Circles:** To represent ripples of energy and vibration.
*   **Gradients:** To create a sense of energy flow and transition.
*   **Glowing Effects:** To give a mystical, energetic aura to our visuals.

## 8. Final Checklist

Before publishing any material, ask yourself:

1.  **Is it on-brand?** Does it align with our voice, visuals, and values?
2.  **Is it high-quality?** Is the design professional and the copy well-written?
